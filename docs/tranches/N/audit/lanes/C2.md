## Verdict

The gradient pane is **structurally sound and the most "house-correct" of the editor panes** — it already uses `font-display` section heads, the glass stop-bar (`glass-subtle`), a real per-interval easing-curve SVG, and a syntax-highlighted live CSS editor. The bones are good. But it is **under-energized against the house design language** (large audacious typography, colorful pops, mathematical motifs, purposeful motion) and carries **three concrete defects**: a dead/unreachable `resolution` parameter, a systemic low-contrast text register that fails legibility on the very captures shipped as evidence, and a flat monochrome easing-curve treatment that wastes the pane's best "math motif" opportunity.

**Scores (1–5, 5 = house-exemplary):**
| Axis | Score | One-line |
|---|---|---|
| Design hierarchy | 3.5 | Clear section rhythm via `font-display` heads + `<hr>`; but the hero preview + bar are two near-identical gradient rectangles stacked (redundant), and CSS section is visually heaviest yet least important. |

## Defects (concrete, evidence-cited)

### C2-1 (P1) — `resolution` is a dead, unreachable parameter
`resolution` is fully threaded: declared `useGradientInterpolation.ts:70` (`ref(32)`), consumed in the coalesced-CSS step count `useGradientCSS.ts:75` (`Math.max(2, Math.round(resolution / (stops.length - 1)))`), and re-exported through `useGradientModel.ts:196`. It controls the fidelity of the eased gradient (how many baked sub-stops). **But there is no UI control to change it** — `grep -rn resolution demo/@/components/custom/gradient` shows it only ever *read*, and *written* exactly once, at `GradientVisualizer.vue:81` (`resolution.value = 32` inside `resetGradient`). The whole easing feature's output quality is hardwired to 32 with no user reach. Either expose it (a slider in the Easing section — natural home, and the pane's marquee "math" knob) or delete the plumbing. **Gestalt fix:** add a "Smoothness / steps" slider beside the per-interval easing rows; it makes the easing feature's effect visible and legitimizes the parameter.

### C2-2 (P1) — Systemic low-contrast text register fails legibility
Three faint registers compound on the glass wash:
- `section-subtitle` (`utils.css:18-27`): `color-mix(... muted-foreground 50% ...)` — the "Left-to-righ…", "Perceptual,…", "Nearest arc" descriptors under TYPE/SPACE/HUE. In `gradient-375-light.png` these are near-invisible.
- The stop-bar hint `text-mono-small text-muted-foreground/40` (`GradientStopEditor.vue:146`) — "Double-click to add · Right-click to remove · Drag to reposition", the **only discoverability affordance for the entire stop-manipulation model**, rendered at 40% opacity. In `gradient-375-dark.png` it's a ghost.
- `text-caption` descriptions inside SelectItems (`GradientVisualizer.vue:154/172/190`) are *additionally* italic (glass-ui typography.css:361).

These are the captures the team shipped as "good" evidence, and the text is unreadable in them. **Gestalt fix:** lift the hint to `text-muted-foreground` (no opacity divisor) and `section-subtitle` to ~70% or drop the `-mix` entirely; truncation already line-clamps, so faintness adds nothing but illegibility.

### C2-3 (P2) — Redundant double gradient hero
`GradientVisualizer.vue:121-126` renders a decorative `h-20 sm:h-24` preview swatch of `coalescedCSS`, then `GradientStopEditor` (line 128) renders a *second* `h-10` bar of the **same `coalescedCSS`** with handles on it. Visible in every 375 capture as two stacked near-identical gradient rectangles. The top one carries no interaction and no extra information the bar doesn't. **Gestalt fix:** delete the separate hero; make the interactive stop-bar itself taller and the hero (e.g. `h-16` with handles below it as a thin rail), or repurpose the hero as the *type/direction* preview (radial/conic look different from the linear bar) so the two surfaces actually differ.

### C2-4 (P2) — Easing curve is a flat monochrome afterthought; the pane's best math motif is wasted
`EasingSelector.vue:56-64` draws a 48×32 SVG easing curve per interval — exactly the "mathematical motif" the house language calls for — but it's hardcoded `stroke="hsl(248, 88%, 71%)"` (a one-off violet, not a design token, not the iconography palette), 48px wide (postage-stamp), with a faint dashed diagonal reference. It never animates when you switch presets. This is the single most house-aligned element in the pane and it's rendered as a tiny gray-bordered thumbnail. **Gestalt fix:** promote one large shared curve visualization for the *selected* interval (sized like the hero, token-stroked with `--primary` or a section-color accent), tween the polyline between presets (`d` morph or opacity-crossfade), and overlay the current-`t` marker animating left→right — turning a static thumbnail into the pane's signature audacious-math surface.

### C2-5 (P2) — Stop handles low-affordance on light stops + no positional readout
Handles are `border-white/80` (`GradientStopEditor.vue:128`) — on a near-white stop they nearly vanish (the leftmost green handle in `gradient-375-light.png` is borderline). There's also no numeric position readout while dragging; `getPosition` rounds to 0.1% but the user never sees the value (the direction slider, by contrast, shows `{{ direction }}°` at `GradientVisualizer.vue:201`). **Gestalt fix:** dual-ring handle (dark+light) for contrast on any background, and a small tooltip/badge showing `position%` on the active/dragging handle (mirrors the direction readout pattern already in the pane).

### C2-6 (P2) — No "audacious typography" or colorful pop; pane is monochrome-muted
Against the house language (large audacious type, colorful audacious pops as in the iconography), the gradient pane's only large type is the `PaneHeader` `text-heading` "Gradient" (`PaneHeader.vue:3`); every in-pane head is `text-subheading text-muted-foreground` (`GradientVisualizer.vue:140,210,227`) — *muted*, ~20px, gray. The gradient itself is the only color in the frame. The `Rainbow` dock icon (viewSchema.ts:138) promises chromatic energy the pane never delivers. **Gestalt fix:** let the section heads carry a section-color accent (glass-ui `--section-color-*` already exists and `section-label--tinted` is built for this, typography.css:501), and consider a larger display-weight numeric readout for stop count / interval count as a math flourish.

## What's already right (keep)
- `font-display` (Fraunces) section heads — correct house head register.
- The glass stop-bar (`glass-subtle`, `GradientStopEditor.vue:109`) + spring handle scale (`--ease-spring`, line 135) — genuine purposeful motion.
- Per-interval easing with a live curve preview — the right *idea*, under-executed (C2-4).
- Bi-directional live CSS editor with hljs syntax highlighting + debounced parse (`GradientCodeEditor.vue`) — a real power-user surface, well built.
- Token-clean: no arbitrary `[var(--…)]`, uses `rounded-card`/`shadow-card`/`glass-subtle` named utilities throughout.

2. **C2-4** (math-motif promotion): one large animated easing curve as the pane's signature surface — also the home for the resolution slider.
3. **C2-1** (resolution): expose as the smoothness slider inside #2, or delete.
4. **C2-3** (dedupe hero): collapse the two gradient rectangles into one interactive surface + a differentiated type/direction preview.
5. **C2-6** (chromatic energy): section-color-tinted heads to honor the `Rainbow` icon promise.
