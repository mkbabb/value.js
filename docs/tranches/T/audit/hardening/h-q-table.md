STOP the whole corpus stands behind. For each Q1–Q17: is the question well-posed? is the DEFAULT

are the alternatives complete and fairly stated? Then the **inverse hunt**: decisions embedded in

## F1 — [MUSTFIX] Q11 bundles two orthogonal decision axes and buries the owner's single most explicit numeric instruction under a mechanics title

**Corpus location**: `T.md:523` = `SYNTHESIS.md:582` (Q11); the burial is compounded by there
being NO Q-row for T-2 (the title ×φ) — it routes to W4-1 (`SYNTHESIS §1.2` T-2) with no
ratification touch-point of its own.

**Evidence.** Q11 is titled **"Readout line-lock levers (T-7 at ×φ)"** and its default answers a
*readout-mechanics* question (per-space integer least-counts, fit clamp, honest 2-line locks for
lab/ictcp/jzazbz). But the pass-2 amendment folded a *second, unrelated* decision into the same
row: *"The ×φ reading itself … the D2 owner-word re-cut of the literal 1.5 — rides this row —
ratifying Q11 ratifies ×φ."* Per D2 (`T.md:121-133`) the ×φ reading governs **three surfaces —
picker title (41.89→67.78px), pane title (25.89→41.89px), AND the readout** — i.e. it re-cuts the
owner's explicit written instruction ("The 'Lab' title should be **1.5×** bigger using our golden
scale", mandate §0) into ×1.618 on the app's most prominent titles, a ~+62% size change. Two
consequences:

1. **Ill-posed by bundling.** The "Live alternatives" cell mixes two axes: `the literal ×1.5`
   is a *size-reading* alternative; `hold lab at 1-decimal one-line` and `blanket 2-line
   everywhere` are *line-lock* alternatives. A single accept/reject on Q11 cannot express "×φ
   size YES, but blanket 2-line" or "literal 1.5× but levers 1+2" — the owner is forced into one
   answer across two independent decisions, one of which (the lab line-lock levers) is deep
   demo-internal type engineering the owner has no basis to adjudicate.
2. **The owner's own number is under-surfaced.** The single most visible re-cut in the tranche —
   reinterpreting the owner's explicit "1.5×" — rides a row whose HEADLINE is readout line-locks
   and whose T-2 (titles) half has no home in the table at all. An owner who rules "Q11:
   default" is silently ratifying the reinterpretation of their own written number without a row
   that foregrounds it. (t-title-typography §1's derivation — |ln1.5−lnφ|=0.076 < |ln1.5−ln√φ|=
   0.165 — is sound; the defect is *presentation at the gate*, not the math.)

**Proposed amendment** (the amend pass owns folding): split Q11 into **Q11a — the ×φ size reading
(the D2 re-cut of "1.5×", governing titles + readout; default ×φ, alt literal ×1.5 vs √φ)** and
**Q11b — the readout line-lock levers (default 1+2, lever 3 for lab-class; alt blanket-2-line vs
lab-fit-down)**. This makes the owner's own number its own ruling and separates it from the type
mechanics — and satisfies lesson-15's "the re-cut is argued in the table the owner rules on."

## F2 — [MUSTFIX] Q5's DEFAULT recommends the un-ratified C3 re-cut *over* the owner's literal instruction, and the default form may not read as "rainbow" at all

**Corpus location**: `T.md:517` = `SYNTHESIS.md:576` (Q5).

**Evidence.** The owner's verbatim line (mandate §0): *"Only 'palletes' should be rainbow."*
Q5's **default** is the **data-strip chip** — "a 5-segment miniature palette (the user's current
palette when present, else the derived guarded fan)". Two problems with the default's
defensibility:

1. **The default contradicts the owner's literal word, resting on a law the owner never
   ratified.** The chip is justified as "color-as-DATA, unifies T-10 with T-17 under the **C3
   law**." But C3 ("color appears only as color DATA; chrome/material/type neutral") is a
   *corpus-invented* doctrine — t-contradictions C3 itself records that "**no single row encodes
   the ONE law + its complete sanctioned-exception ledger**," and it is nowhere put to the owner
   as a question (see F4). So the DEFAULT overrides the owner's explicit "rainbow" by appeal to an
   un-ratified law. The pass-2 clause — "the ratification package LEADS with the owner-verbatim
   letterform reading, chip second as the recommendation" — is a *prose* mitigation that lives
   outside the default cell; the table (which the owner rules on) still bolds the chip as the
   recommendation. An owner who accepts defaults inverts their own instruction.
2. **The default may not even be a rainbow.** "the user's current palette when present" is
   whatever hues the user saved — 5 blues render 5 blues. The one option that reliably reads as
   the owner's rainbow is the *alternative* (the guarded derived analogous ramp on the
   letterforms — the contrast-safe descendant of the pre-S `pastel-rainbow-text` the owner
   remembers, t-nav-dropdowns F3). Recommending the option *least* faithful to "rainbow" as the
   default, over the option that IS the guarded rainbow, is the inversion the pass-2 amenders half-
   caught but did not carry into the default column.

**Proposed amendment**: make Q5 a genuine either/or with the owner-verbatim letterform as a
CO-EQUAL (or the recommended) reading — the table default should not recommend against the owner's
explicit word by appeal to C3. At minimum, move the "leads with the owner-verbatim letterform"
clause INTO the DEFAULT cell as the presented-first option, and state plainly that the chip is the
C3-motivated re-cut the owner may decline. (Keep the accessibility constraint that killed the
fixed-pastel rainbow — the ramp must be derived + guarded, per Q4-EXCISE's own record.)

## F3 — [SHOULDFIX] Inverse hunt: two owner-word re-cuts settled silently in the doctrine with NO ratification row (lesson-15 applied unevenly)

**Corpus location**: (a) the blob z-law — `T.md:193` = `SYNTHESIS.md:209` (D8) + `SYNTHESIS §3
W4-5:321`; source `t-blob-hero.md:141-142`. (b) the boot fade — `SYNTHESIS.md:154` (B2) + `:255`
(t-aurora §2.1 note) + `SYNTHESIS §3 W2-2:293`.

**Evidence.** Lesson 15 (`T.md:490-492`) is the law: "Owner-word re-cuts surface for ratification,
never settle silently." It was honored for T-5 ("little glass card"→WELL, Q4), T-2 ("1.5×"→×φ,
Q11), the Palettes "rainbow"→chip (Q5), and the chrome slice (Q10). Two re-cuts of the same class
did NOT reach the table:

- **"higher z than all" → "below floating chrome."** The owner (mandate §0, T-8): the blob "should
  have a **higher z than all**." D8 narrows this: `--z-ornament` = "top of the CONTENT stack
  (chrome stays above; the two readings of 'all' never conflict in paint)." The authoring lane
  itself labels this an **"Interpretation note for ratification"** (t-blob-hero §2 z-law) — it
  *asks* to be ratified — yet no Q captures it. Q3 covers only flush-vs-corner-kiss. An owner who
  said "higher z than ALL" and finds the blob paints *under* the dock has had their explicit word
  quietly narrowed with no ruling.
- **The boot fade DOUBLED (0.45s→0.9s) in response to the owner's literal "too slow."** The owner
  (§0.3): the loading animations "Are too gray/**slow**/jittery." The doctrine (D3 / t-aurora
  §2.1) *lengthens* the field fade from 0.45s to 0.9s — "the current fade is HALF the story too
  fast." This is a defensible nuance (slow = the freeze/pileup, F-5; the fade itself is too fast),
  but it is a design decision that runs *opposite* to the owner's literal word, embedded in
  doctrine + W2-2 with no ratification touch-point. Q2 covers only the C/H variance bracket
  (T-26), never T-27's timing/curve. The owner may be surprised to learn "too slow" was answered,
  in part, by making an animation slower.

**Proposed amendment**: add two rows (or annexes to Q3 and Q2): **the blob z-interpretation**
("all" = content-top-below-chrome vs literally-above-everything — the lane already framed it "for
ratification") and **the boot fade/timing re-cut for T-27** (0.9s derive-in + the 200ms @property
transition + palette-ease, presented as the bracket W8 will judge — surfaced now because it
re-reads the owner's "too slow"). Both are the exact "argue the re-cut in the table" shape lesson
15 mandates.

## F4 — [SHOULDFIX] Inverse hunt: the C3 "color = data only" law + its whole exception ledger is a corpus-invented doctrine that overrides several owner findings, but only its narrowest slice (Q10) is ratified

**Corpus location**: the law lives in D1 corollary (`T.md:116-118`), `SYNTHESIS §1.2 T-24` row,
and t-contradictions C3 (`:339-362`); the ONLY ratification touch-point is **Q10** (`T.md:522`),
scoped to Tools/Login chrome alone.

**Evidence.** The owner's words are "The rest should be **white/black**" (T-10) and "consistent
**gray/black/white** colors" (T-24). The corpus reads these as the C3 LAW — "color appears only as
color DATA; chrome, material, and type are neutral" — plus a **sanctioned-exception ledger** that
carves out, from the owner's neutrality instruction, at least eight surface classes: ramps, dots,
palette strips/chips, the gamut netting (T-6, hue-carrying), the aurora field (T-26), the blob,
the Palettes-row rainbow, and admin-gold. t-contradictions C3 is explicit that this ledger is
"stated PIECEMEAL across three lanes" and that "**no single row encodes the ONE law + its complete
sanctioned-exception ledger**." Deciding which surfaces are "data" (exempt) vs "chrome" (neutral)
is a taste judgment with real latitude — e.g. is the gamut netting "color data"? is admin-gold in
or out? — and it is the interpretive frame under which *six* other owner findings (T-6/10/17/26,
T-13/19, T-8) keep their color despite T-24. Yet the owner is asked to ratify only the single
chrome edge (Q10). The core law and its exception ledger settle by derivation, not by ruling —
the same taste-by-proxy shape the tranche's meta-finding (t-contradictions §5) exists to prevent.

**Proposed amendment**: promote the C3 law + its complete exception ledger to a ratification row
(the owner confirms "color = data only, chrome/material/type neutral" AND the exact
sanctioned-exception set), with Q10's chrome question as a sub-clause. Encoding the whole ledger
where the owner rules is what makes T-24 enforceable (t-contradictions C3's own ask) and stops the
next audit re-opening whichever exemption the corpus never surfaced.
