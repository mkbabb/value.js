## Findings

### F1 — [MUSTFIX] "white/black" (T-10) + "gray/black/white" (T-24) re-cut to the WARM cream/brown neutral family + stone-ink, raw #fff/#000 explicitly REFUSED, with NO Q and NO ledger row

**The re-cut.** The owner's two literal color instructions —
- T-10: *"The rest should be **white/black**."*
- T-24: *"Do a full audit of all glass card areas to use consistent **gray/black/white** colors."*

— are both realized NOT as white, black, or gray, but as the app's **warm cream/brown**
neutral family, and the corpus explicitly retires raw `#fff`/`#000`:
- `t-card-material.md:75` (the D1 doctrine box): *"One neutral family (the house **warm
  cream/brown** `--card`/`--muted`/`--background`), grays only as `--foreground` tone-mixes,
  **raw `#fff`/`#000` only inside color-math ramps.**"*
- `SYNTHESIS.md:131` (D1 RETIRES): *"… raw `bg-black`/`bg-white` …"* — the corpus deletes the
  owner's exact "white/black" surface.
- `t-nav-dropdowns.md:68-73` (T-10 cure): the menu ink is the *"measured `rgb(28,25,23)` /
  `rgb(233,230,226)` **stone-ink**"* — warm-toned near-white/near-black, not pure #fff/#000;
  reinforced at `:249` ("the ink menu's white/black must key to the same neutral ladder …
  the measured stone pair is today's de-facto ink").
- The "ONE neutral family" is asserted as doctrine at `SYNTHESIS.md:132` / `T.md:115`, never
  put to the owner.

**Why it is a lesson-15 violation.** This is exactly the class the corpus's own law governs
(T.md:490-492): a literal owner word re-read to a different concrete thing. Reading "monochrome"
into "white/black" is defensible — but the SPECIFIC choice of a **warm** cream/brown family
(over neutral gray, or over the owner's own literal "gray/black/white") is a live taste decision
the owner explicitly gestured at with the word "gray." Every sibling re-cut of this class got a
Q the owner rules on (glass-card→WELL/Q4, rainbow→chip/Q5, 1.5→×φ/Q11). This one — the largest
single surface class in the tranche (T-24 = "ALL glass card areas") — settled silently. The one
Q that touches the sentence, **Q10** (`SYNTHESIS.md:581`), rules only the SCOPE ("does chrome
fall under 'the rest white/black'"), never the FORM (warm vs neutral, stone-ink vs literal).

**Proposed amendment.** Add a ratification row (or an explicit C3-exception-ledger annotation
at SYNTHESIS §2 D1 / T-24's §1.2 row) surfacing: *"'white/black' (T-10) + 'gray/black/white'
(T-24) → the house **warm** cream/brown neutral family + stone-ink `rgb(28,25,23)`/
`rgb(233,230,226)`; raw #fff/#000 retired. An owner-word re-cut — the owner named 'gray',
'white', 'black'; the corpus lands warm neutrals. DEFAULT: warm family (matches the app's
existing atmosphere/`--card` tone). ALT: neutral-gray family per the owner's literal 'gray'."*
Mark AMENDED, route it beside Q10 or as a new Q.

  left'"*. Grammatically "moved … down and to the left" IS relative motion, so the inward-from-
  top-right reading is the more correct one and it is ARGUED (not silent) — hence a Note, not a

  entire W4-5 seat law rests on it. Worth one clause in Q3's DEFAULT confirming the corner.

  via R7 doctrine (`SYNTHESIS.md:56`) not a Q. Defensible (error ≠ "absent content is a
  palette"), but it is a narrowing of the owner's literal "all cases"; O-9 ("all-cases oracle")

## Bottom line

The corpus's owner-word re-cut discipline (lesson-15) is REAL and mostly applied — but the
ledger it relies on is both **incomplete** (misses white/black+gray/black/white → warm
neutrals; misses blob-z "all") and **self-inconsistent** (§9 "two" vs lesson-15/PROGRESS
"three"). The single most consequential omission is F1: the owner's "white/black" and
"gray/black/white" — the exact words governing the tranche's largest surface class (T-24 = ALL
glass cards) — land as a WARM cream/brown family with raw #fff/#000 refused, a taste decision
the owner never ratified. Fold F1 into the re-cut ledger, surface it as a Q, correct the §9
count, and answer "wildly re-structured", and the owner-word map handed to ratification becomes
complete.
